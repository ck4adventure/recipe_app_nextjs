## Flax Wheat Bread Recipe

**Makes 1 loaf (12-14 slices)**

### Ingredients:
- 2 cups whole wheat flour (or 1¬Ω cups whole wheat + ¬Ω cup all-purpose)
- ¬Ω cup ground flaxseed
- 1¬Ω tsp active dry yeast
- 1 Tbsp honey
- 1 tsp salt
- 1¬º cups warm water (110¬∞F)
- 2 Tbsp olive oil
- 2 Tbsp whole flax seeds (for topping)

### Directions:

1. **Activate yeast:** In a small bowl, combine warm water, honey, and yeast. Let sit 5-10 minutes until foamy.

2. **Mix dry ingredients:** In a large bowl, whisk together flour, ground flaxseed, and salt.

3. **Combine:** Pour yeast mixture and olive oil into dry ingredients. Stir until a shaggy dough forms.

4. **Knead:** Turn out onto a floured surface and knead 8-10 minutes until smooth and elastic (or use a stand mixer with dough hook for 5-6 minutes).

5. **First rise:** Place in an oiled bowl, cover with a damp towel, and let rise in a warm spot for 1 hour or until doubled.

6. **Shape:** Punch down dough, shape into a loaf, and place in a greased 9x5" loaf pan. Brush top with water and sprinkle with whole flax seeds.

7. **Second rise:** Cover and let rise 30-45 minutes until dough rises above the pan edge.

8. **Bake:** Preheat oven to 375¬∞F. Bake 35-40 minutes until golden and hollow-sounding when tapped. Internal temp should reach 190¬∞F.

9. **Cool:** Remove from pan and cool completely on a wire rack before slicing.

**Storage:** Keeps 4-5 days at room temperature, or slice and freeze for up to 3 months.

---

## Assembly

1. **Toast the bread lightly** (optional but recommended‚Äîbrings out the nuttiness).

2. **Spread mustard** on both slices.

3. **Layer bottom slice:** Arugula first (protects bread from getting soggy), then turkey, then brie.

4. **Add extras:** If using apple slices, tuck them between turkey and brie. If using fig jam, spread it on the brie before closing.

5. **Top and press gently.** Cut in half on the diagonal because it matters.

---

## Variations

**Savory Depth:** Add caramelized onions and swap Dijon for whole grain mustard with herbs

**Protein Boost:** Add 1 sliced hard-boiled egg (+70 cal, +6g protein)

**What it provides:** Complete plant protein from lentils, a full day's fiber in one meal, calcium from tahini and feta, anti-inflammatory fats, and a spectrum of vitamins A, C, K, and folate.

## Ingredients (serves 1 generously, or 2 moderate portions)

### The Bowl Base:
- 1 cup cooked green or brown lentils (230 cal)
- 1 cup cauliflower florets (55 cal)
- 1 cup carrots, halved lengthwise (80 cal)
- ¬Ω cup red bell pepper strips (25 cal)
- 1 Tbsp olive oil for roasting (120 cal)

### Toppings:
- 2 Tbsp tahini (180 cal)
- ¬º cup crumbled feta (100 cal)
- 2 Tbsp toasted pumpkin or sunflower seeds (100 cal)
- Fresh parsley or cilantro
- Lemon wedge
- Pinch of za'atar or cumin

### Optional Grain:
- ¬Ω cup cooked farro or bulgur wheat (+110 cal)

### For the Tahini Drizzle:
- 3 Tbsp tahini
- 2 Tbsp lemon juice
- 1 small garlic clove, minced

- Pinch of salt
- Optional: ¬º tsp cumin or smoked paprika

---

## Sunday Afternoon Batch Prep (Makes 4-5 Bowls)

**Total active time:** 20 minutes  
**Total time:** 1 hour

### Step 1: Cook the Lentils (15 min active, 25 min total)

1. Rinse 2 cups dry lentils (green or brown‚Äîthey hold their shape better).
2. Combine in a pot with 5 cups water or broth and a bay leaf.
3. Bring to a boil, reduce to simmer, cook 20-25 minutes until tender but not mushy.
4. Drain, remove bay leaf, season with salt and a drizzle of olive oil.
5. **Store:** Refrigerate in an airtight container for up to 5 days, or freeze portions for up to 3 months.

### Step 2: Roast the Vegetables (10 min active, 30-35 min total)

1. **Preheat oven to 425¬∞F.** Line 2 large baking sheets with parchment.

2. **Prep vegetables:**
   - 4 cups cauliflower florets
   - 4 cups carrots (halved lengthwise, then cut into 3" pieces)
   - 2 cups red bell pepper strips
   - Optional: Brussels sprouts, butternut squash, beets

3. **Toss each vegetable separately** with olive oil (about 1 Tbsp per 2 cups veg), salt, and pepper. Keep them separate on the pans‚Äîthey cook at different rates.

4. **Roasting times:**
   - Carrots: 30-35 minutes (flip halfway)
   - Cauliflower: 25-30 minutes (flip halfway)
   - Bell peppers: 20-25 minutes

5. **Look for:** Deep caramelization and charred edges. Don't be shy‚Äîthis is where the flavor lives.

6. **Store:** Refrigerate in separate containers for up to 5 days.

### Step 3: Make the Tahini Drizzle (5 minutes)

1. Whisk together tahini, lemon juice, minced garlic, and salt.
2. Add water 1 tablespoon at a time until it's pourable but still thick.
3. Taste and adjust‚Äîmore lemon for brightness, more garlic for punch.
4. **Store:** In a jar in the fridge for up to 2 weeks. It will thicken‚Äîjust stir in water to loosen.

### Step 4: Toast the Seeds (3 minutes)

1. Heat a dry skillet over medium heat.
2. Add pumpkin or sunflower seeds and toast 3-4 minutes, stirring constantly, until fragrant and lightly golden.
3. **Store:** In an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 weeks.

---

## Daily Assembly (5 minutes)

2. **Reheat vegetables** if desired (1 minute in microwave, or eat at room temp).
3. **Build the bowl:** Lentils as base, arrange vegetables around, sprinkle with feta and seeds.
4. **Drizzle generously** with tahini sauce.
5. **Finish:** Fresh herbs, squeeze of lemon, crack of pepper, pinch of za'atar.

## Crispy Roasted Chickpeas (Lentil Substitute)

### Why Swap?
Chickpeas give you that addictive crispy-crunchy texture while maintaining the plant protein. They're slightly higher in calories but also more satisfying. Perfect if you want the bowl to feel more like a "salad with croutons" vibe.

### Nutritional Comparison (per 1 cup cooked):
- **Lentils:** 230 cal, 18g protein, 16g fiber
- **Chickpeas (before roasting):** 270 cal, 15g protein, 12g fiber
- **Add roasting oil:** +120 cal (1 Tbsp olive oil for the batch)

**Per serving (‚Öî cup roasted chickpeas):** ~210 cal, 10g protein

### Ingredients (makes 3 servings)
- 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, or 2 cups cooked from dry
- 1 Tbsp olive oil
- ¬Ω tsp salt
- Spices (choose your flavor profile below)

### Directions

1. **Dry them thoroughly** (this is the secret to crispiness):
   - Drain and rinse chickpeas
   - Pat completely dry with paper towels or a clean kitchen towel
   - Let them air dry for 10-15 minutes if you have time
   - Optional: Remove loose skins (they get extra crispy without them, but not necessary)

2. **Preheat oven to 400¬∞F.** Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

3. **Toss with oil and salt.** Spread in a single layer‚Äîdon't overcrowd or they'll steam instead of crisp.

4. **Roast 25-35 minutes:**
   - Shake the pan every 10 minutes for even browning
   - They're done when golden-brown and crunchy on the outside
   - They'll crisp up more as they cool

5. **Add spices in the last 2-3 minutes** (or toss after removing from oven). Adding too early can cause burning.

6. **Cool completely before storing.** They'll continue to crisp as they cool.

### Flavor Profiles

**Za'atar & Lemon (Middle Eastern):**
- 1 tsp za'atar
- ¬Ω tsp garlic powder
- Zest of ¬Ω lemon

**Smoky Paprika:**
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- ¬Ω tsp cumin
- ¬º tsp cayenne (optional)

**Curry Spiced:**
- 1 tsp curry powder
- ¬Ω tsp turmeric
- ¬º tsp ginger powder

**Simply Garlic & Herb:**
- 1 tsp garlic powder
- ¬Ω tsp dried oregano or rosemary
- Fresh cracked black pepper

**Sweet & Savory (Autumn):**
- ¬Ω tsp cinnamon
- ¬º tsp nutmeg
- Pinch of cayenne
- (Add 1 tsp maple syrup with the oil‚Äîwatch closely, they can burn)

### Storage & Reheating

**Storage:** Store in an airtight container at room temperature for 2-3 days. They will lose some crispiness in the fridge.

**Recrisp:** Spread on a baking sheet and reheat at 350¬∞F for 5-7 minutes.

**Meal prep note:** For best texture, roast chickpeas the same day or day before eating. They're still good after 3 days, just chewier rather than crunchy.

### Pro Tips

- **The drier, the crispier:** This is non-negotiable. Wet chickpeas = steamed chickpeas = sad chickpeas.
- **Don't overcrowd:** Use two pans if needed.
- **Parchment is your friend:** Prevents sticking and makes cleanup easy.
- **Be patient:** They need the full roasting time. Under-roasted chickpeas are chalky.
- **Eat them warm:** They're at peak crispiness right out of the oven.

### Using in the Bowl

Replace the lentils entirely with ‚Öî-1 cup roasted chickpeas, or use half lentils/half chickpeas for textural variety. The crispy chickpeas add a "crouton" element that makes the bowl feel more dynamic.

They also make an incredible snack on their own‚Äîdouble or triple the batch and thank yourself later.

## **Quinoa Power Bowl Variation**

**Why Quinoa?**
Quinoa is a complete protein (all 9 essential amino acids), cooks faster than lentils, and has a light, fluffy texture that feels less heavy. Perfect if you want the bowl to feel more like a grain bowl than a legume bowl.

**Nutritional Comparison (per 1 cup cooked):**
- **Lentils:** 230 cal, 18g protein, 16g fiber
- **Quinoa:** 220 cal, 8g protein, 5g fiber

**To maintain protein:** Use 1 cup cooked quinoa + ¬Ω cup cooked lentils, or add an extra egg/more feta

**Cooking Quinoa (makes 3 cups cooked):**

1. **Rinse 1 cup dry quinoa** in a fine-mesh strainer (removes bitter coating)
2. Combine with 2 cups water or broth in a pot
3. Bring to a boil, reduce to low simmer, cover
4. Cook 15 minutes until water is absorbed
5. Remove from heat, let sit covered 5 minutes
6. Fluff with a fork

**Storage:** Refrigerate up to 5 days, or freeze portions up to 3 months

**Flavor boost:** Cook quinoa in vegetable broth, or add a bay leaf and garlic clove to the cooking water

**Assembly tip:** Quinoa absorbs dressing beautifully‚Äîtoss it with half the tahini sauce while still warm, then add vegetables and remaining sauce on top.
